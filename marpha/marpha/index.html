Our Tradition

At Marpha Thakali Bhancha Ghar, we have preserved the authentic taste of Thakali cuisine for generations, rooted in the culinary heritage of Nepal's Thak Khola region. Our dishes are a celebration of tradition, crafted with time-honored recipes passed down through families, blending the robust flavors of Himalayan spices with the warmth of our hospitality.

Every plate tells a story of the Thakali people, who mastered the art of creating nourishing meals for traders and travelers crossing the rugged Himalayan trails. From our signature Dhedo to the aromatic Thak Khola Aalu, we bring you the essence of Nepal’s culinary legacy, served with love and authenticity.

Origins of Thakali Khana

Thakali cuisine originates from the Thak Khola region of Nepal, developed by the Thakali people who controlled vital salt trade routes between Tibet and India. Our recipes preserve these ancient culinary traditions that evolved to sustain travelers and traders crossing the Himalayas.

Our 40-Year Journey

1983: Humble Beginnings

Founded by Bhim Parsad Lalchan in Marpha village with just 4 tables

1990: Mugling Expansion

Opened first city branch in Mugling, introducing Thakali cuisine to tourists

2016: National Recognition

Awarded "Best Traditional Restaurant" by Nepal Tourism Board

2023: 40th Anniversary

Now serving 2nd generation customers with 12 locations nationwide
